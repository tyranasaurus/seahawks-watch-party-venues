Cost estimates are all-in for 30 people over a ~3-hour game (reservation/room + food & beverage), excluding WA tax (~10.1%) and 18–22% service charge. NFL Sundays run higher. NFL audio = can they play the game's sound (not just the picture) — many sports bars only run audio for one featured game, and some breweries have no sound system at all, so confirm it for every venue. Experience rating (x/10) scores the watch-party we actually want: a comfortable, private or semi-private space that's still exciting with the drinks flowing. 10 = your own (or roped-off) room, big screens with game audio, plenty of drinks on tap, and room to get loud — lounge / couch seating is a plus, not a minus (comfy can still be rowdy). Points come off for small or single screens, no/limited audio, beer-only taprooms, or a sleepy room with nothing to rally around — not for being comfortable.
